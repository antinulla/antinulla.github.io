about me
I am a senior researcher currently active in the field of Cognitive Neuroscience. My background is in Physics and Cosmology. I got my first PhD in Astrophysics and I kept working in the field for fews years before the big change! Since then I have been active in the fields of multusensory perception, motor control and cognitive processes such as planning and decision-making. I have acquired a unique blend of experimental and theoretical skills. I use a combination of immersive VR, motion capture, physiological assessment and computational models to study how agents builds up the perception of their own body and control their movement to interact with real and virtual environments. I am also interested in how humans interact with new technologies and how this shapes our minds and our behavior.
I have personally run over 50 studies on hundreds of people and supervised and co-supervised over 10 students and postdocs. In my practice I focus on unveiling meaningful patterns out of complexity, and once I gain new insights into something I love to find the ways to synthesize them and make this actionable for others.
Areas of Interest

SELF-PERCEPTION and VIRTUAL AVATARS

How our brains build up the perception of our own body based on the continuous stream of information arriving through different sensory channels? And, how does this multisensory integration processing shapes our interactions within immersive virtual environments? In my studies I tackle these questions by inducing body ownership illusions over virtual avatars. I investigate how both perception and behavior may be affected when moving beyond the spatiotemporal constraints of the physical world. At the same time, I am interested at exploring how such insight may inspire improved designs for VR technologies.

RICH BEHAVIOR

The study of complex naturalistic behavior is often beyond the scope of most laboratory studies, because the need for experimental control limit the range of behaviors that can be expressed while preserving such control. We are actively working in developing methodological approachs that overcome these limitations. I combine full-body motion tracking and VR thecnologies with the designed of customized analysis algorithms and the support of theoretical and computational models. In a line of studies, we developed an approach for describing full-body actions with low dimensional, still accurate, quantitative representations (i) for comparison accross action repetitions and across individuals, and (ii) for characterizing the information about the action's outcome embedded in the action excution. This in turn allow to design principled experiments to study the naturalistic (non-verbal) interaction between two or more agents.
I am furhter interested in the principled designed and study of embodied decisions in naturalistic conditions. I have starting exploring this in the context of sport climbing.
